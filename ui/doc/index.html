Doshlogger

Simple bank account checker to match receipts against statements.

Introduction

Every month I diligently go through all my collected receipts and reference them against my bank statements. It's a pain in the proverbials trying to match each transaction against the scraps of paper I collect.

And then Natwest made the statements available for download, in numerous formats, including CSV. The format of this is a complete mess, but eventually it made sufficient sense for me to build a pretty gui tools to import the statements and track everything in a database.

And hence was born doshlogger.

The basic idea is that each receipt or direct debit can be easily found and validated against the bank statement - and flagged as checked. Eventally, you want to concentrate on the transactions for which you have no record. Are these valid transactions? Has someone beenm dipping into your account? Has a shop put your credit card through twice? Have you been over charged? Has a direct debit suddenly taken a hike?

Basically, by sifting out valid transations, you can focus on the rest.

doshlogger will import bank statements into a database which can be MySql, Postgres or sqlite. The type of statements is can understand is limited to the formats I have encountered - NatWest, Cater Allen, Credit Agricole & Paypal You can easily add new accounts that match your own bank statements.

Once imported, the transactions are presented to you and once you are happy that a transaction is ok, just mark it as valid -  or as checked.

There are numerous filter options that will, for example, only show transactions from a certain bank, a date range, a format type, an amount - which can enable you to quickly identify a certain transaction.
